Descriptive statistics

Descriptive statistics is again divided into Measures of Central tendancy and Measures of Variability

Measures of Central tendancy

Central tendancy: It gives an idea about the concentration of the values(obeservations). A Measures of Central
tendancy is statistical average or single value which represents entire distribution

It includes: Mean Median Mode Geometric Mean Harmonic Mean

Arithmetic Mean: Mean = Average of set of values:

    It has statistical series which are divided into individual series, discrete series and Continuous series
  • Individual series is further divided into direct method and shortcut method
  • Direct method: Sum of all individual observation/ No of individual obeservations
  • Shortcut Method: X̅ = A + ∑d/N
    here ∑d = X-A Derivation of item of assumed mean

Rules for writting short-cut method:
  • Take any one item in the series as assumed mean A.
  • Take the deviation of item from assumed mean
  • Obtain Sum of devation
  • Submit values

    • Discrete series:X̅ = ∑fx/N
    Here f = frequency,
    Short-cut method X̅ = A + ∑fd/N

    Continuous Series: X̅ = ∑fm/N
